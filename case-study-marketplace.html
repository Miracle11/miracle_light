Marketplace – Wellness & Beauty Booking Platform

A centralized platform connecting customers with wellness and beauty businesses across Nigeria.

Lead Product Designer 2024–2025 Web + Mobile

Project Overview

Marketplace is a centralized platform connecting customers with wellness and beauty businesses across Nigeria (spas, barbershops, hair salons, etc.). It enables location‑based discovery, real‑time booking with staff selection, secure escrow payments, and business management tools. The platform serves three user types: customers, business owners, and platform administrators.

Customer webview – booking flow

My Role

As Lead Product Designer, I owned the end‑to‑end design process – from user research and information architecture to high‑fidelity UI and interaction design. I collaborated with the backend team on API requirements and delivered a responsive web design using Tailwind CSS and Lucide icons, ensuring consistency with the Flutter mobile app.

The Challenge

The Nigerian wellness industry is fragmented. Customers struggle to find reliable providers, check real‑time availability, or pay securely. Business owners lack online visibility and efficient booking tools. The goal was to build a trusted, scalable platform that streamlines the entire booking‑to‑payment journey for all stakeholders.

Design Process

1. Discovery & Research

Conducted interviews with spa owners and customers in Lagos to understand pain points (trust, scheduling conflicts, payment assurance). Analyzed competitors (e.g., Airbnb model for services) to identify opportunities.

2. Information Architecture

Mapped three core user flows: Customer (Search → Select service → Choose staff → Book → Pay → Review); Business Owner (Register → Onboard → Pending verification → Manage bookings); Admin (Approve businesses → Monitor transactions → Resolve disputes). Structured features into 17 epics, prioritizing MVP.

3. Visual Design

Mobile‑first responsive design with Tailwind CSS, ensuring a seamless experience on phones, tablets, and desktops. Primary color: #f97316 (orange) for CTAs and highlights, paired with neutral whites and grays for clarity. Typography: Inter font family for excellent readability. Shadow cards instead of borders to create a fresh, modern card‑based interface. Lucide icons for consistent, recognizable visuals.

4. Key Screens & Interactions

  • Onboarding wizard for businesses with step‑by‑step progress and a pending‑verification dashboard (can edit profile but not accept bookings).
  • Customer homepage: Location‑based search, category grid, featured businesses, and trending services.
  • My Bookings screen with tabbed navigation (Upcoming, History, Cancelled) and one‑click calendar integration (Google, Outlook, Yahoo, iCal).
  • Business settings page with three core sections (User Profile, Business Settings, Notifications) and sidebar navigation for additional options like Security and Payments.
  • Escrow payment flow with conditional release based on business preferences (before/after service).

Business owner dashboard – manage bookings and staff

Key Design Decisions

Trust first

Prominent verification badges and a clear pending state for new businesses build user confidence.

Real‑time availability

Duration‑based scheduling with buffer time prevents double‑booking – visual feedback shows customers exactly when slots are free.

Staff management

Customers can pick a preferred staff member or let the system auto‑assign based on skill and availability; staff profiles include ratings and photos.

Calendar integration

After booking, users can add the appointment to their preferred calendar – a small detail that reduces no‑shows.

Mobile‑first

Given Nigeria’s mobile‑dominant usage, the design prioritizes touch targets, bottom navigation, and responsive layouts.

Lessons Learned

  • User trust is paramount – verification and escrow were non‑negotiable.
  • Staff management adds complexity but is a key differentiator for customers.
  • Clear status indicators (pending, confirmed, completed) reduce support queries.
  • Iterative development (monolith → microservices) allows scaling with market growth.
